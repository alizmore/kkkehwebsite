About KAIF

What KAIF is, exactly.

KAIF draws its universe from the top-performing holdings inside ETFs managed by the world's leading firms: BlackRock, Vanguard, and others. What institutional analysts have already identified as high-quality, the fund inherits.

What AI does is go further. Algorithmic models then evaluate each holding across market capitalization and financial metrics, reweighting positions to maximize risk-adjusted performance. Every asset is selected from within a Shariah-compliant investment universe. The result is a portfolio that combines institutional-grade research, AI-driven optimization, and faith-based ethics, across equities, gold, and Sukuk.

How It Works

A systematic process. Applied at every layer.

KAIF follows a disciplined, AI-driven investment process from source to portfolio — with Shariah compliance embedded throughout.

01
Source from the World's Best ETFs

KAIF identifies top-performing holdings from globally recognized ETFs managed by BlackRock, Vanguard, and other institutional-grade firms. We start where the world's best analysts stop.

02
AI-Driven Screening & Weighting

Algorithmic models evaluate each holding using market cap, momentum, and financial quality metrics. AI reweights allocations across equities, gold, and Sukuk to maximize risk-adjusted performance.

03
Built Within a Shariah-Compliant Universe

Compliance is structural, not a filter applied after selection. Every asset class, every instrument, every position is selected from within a predefined Shariah-compliant universe — excluding prohibited activities, excessive debt ratios, and non-compliant income streams by design.

04
Auto-Rebalancing Aligned to Markets

KAIF monitors market conditions continuously and rebalances allocations automatically. No manual intervention required. No emotional overrides. Just disciplined, systematic execution.

ETF Risks. Strategies that invest in exchange-traded funds (ETFs) are subject to the risks of the underlying securities held by those ETFs. ETF shares may trade at a premium or discount to net asset value. Brokerage commissions and fund expenses apply and will reduce returns.

Fund Manager Discretion. Keheilan Asset Management retains discretion over portfolio construction, instrument selection, rebalancing, and exit decisions. Strategy descriptions represent current methodology and may be updated at manager discretion without prior notice to investors.

Principal Risk Factors. Investing involves risk of loss. The following risks may apply depending on the strategy:

  • Emerging Markets Risk: Investments in emerging market securities involve greater risk than developed market investments, including political instability, currency risk, limited liquidity, and less developed regulatory frameworks.
  • Non-Diversification Risk: A concentrated portfolio may be subject to greater volatility than a broadly diversified portfolio. Loss of any single position may have a material adverse effect on portfolio returns.
  • Equity Market Risk: Equity securities are subject to price volatility driven by macroeconomic conditions, industry factors, and issuer-specific events. There is no guarantee that equity investments will appreciate in value.
  • Concentration Risk: Portfolios focused on particular sectors, geographies, or asset classes may experience greater volatility than more diversified strategies.
  • Sharia-Compliant Investing Risk: Strategies that restrict investments to Shariah-compliant securities may underperform broader market indices or conventional strategies. The universe of eligible securities is smaller, which may limit diversification and liquidity.
  • ETF Structure Risk: ETF liquidity depends on the liquidity of underlying holdings. In stressed market conditions, ETF bid-ask spreads may widen and execution at stated prices may not be possible.

SFDR Disclosure — Article 8 Classification

Keheilan Asset Management is registered and headquartered in the Netherlands and is subject to the EU Sustainable Finance Disclosure Regulation (SFDR) (EU) 2019/2088.

Article 8 Classification. Keheilan's asset management strategies promote social and ethical characteristics within the meaning of Article 8 of SFDR. All strategies are constructed within a Shariah-compliant investment universe — an approach that promotes ethical principles including the exclusion of interest-bearing instruments, alcohol, tobacco, weapons, and gambling-related activities.

How Social Characteristics Are Promoted. The Shariah-compliant nature of the investment universe is structural: instruments are selected from within a predefined universe of Shariah-compliant securities. This is not a post-hoc screen — it is the foundation of instrument selection across all strategies.

No Minimum Sustainable Investment. Keheilan does not commit to a minimum proportion of investments aligned with the EU Taxonomy or with the definition of "sustainable investments" under Article 2(17) of SFDR. The firm promotes social and ethical characteristics through Shariah-compliance but does not represent its strategies as "sustainable investments" in the regulatory sense.

Principal Adverse Impacts. Keheilan does not currently consider principal adverse impacts (PAIs) on sustainability factors at the entity level, as defined under Article 4 of SFDR. This position will be reviewed as the firm's AUM and regulatory obligations evolve.
