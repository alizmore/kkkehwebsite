About KAIF

What KAIF is, exactly.

KAIF draws from top-performing holdings in the global stock exchange. AI identifies which assets to select, how to weight them, and how to construct the portfolio to maximize risk-adjusted returns — all within a Shariah-compliant investment universe.

The process is systematic: AI screens global equities for quality and performance, assigns weights based on financial metrics and market conditions, and builds the portfolio from the ground up. No ETF wrappers. Direct exposure to the global stock market, governed by Shariah throughout.

How It Works

A systematic process. Applied at every layer.

KAIF follows a disciplined, AI-driven investment process from source to portfolio — with Shariah compliance embedded throughout.

01
Source from the World's Best Assets

KAIF identifies top-performing Shariah-compliant assets from across global markets. We start where the world's best investment managers stop.

02
AI-Driven Screening & Weighting

Algorithmic models evaluate each holding using market cap, momentum, and financial quality metrics. AI reweights allocations across equities, gold, and Sukuk to maximize risk-adjusted performance.

03
Built Within a Shariah-Compliant Universe

Compliance is structural, not a filter applied after selection. Every asset class, every instrument, every position is selected from within a predefined Shariah-compliant universe — excluding prohibited activities, excessive debt ratios, and non-compliant income streams by design.

04
AI Portfolio Construction

AI constructs the full portfolio — deciding the composition, the weights, and the structure. No manual intervention. No emotional overrides. Systematic, disciplined execution at every step.

SFDR Disclosure — Article 8 Classification

Keheilan Asset Management is registered in the Netherlands and subject to SFDR (EU) 2019/2088. All strategies are classified as Article 8 — they promote social and ethical characteristics by constructing portfolios within a Shariah-compliant investment universe (excluding interest-bearing instruments, alcohol, tobacco, weapons, and gambling). This is a structural feature of instrument selection, not a post-hoc screen. Keheilan does not commit to a minimum proportion of EU Taxonomy-aligned investments and does not represent its strategies as "sustainable investments" under Article 2(17) of SFDR.
